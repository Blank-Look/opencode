# IT Governance

IT Governance is the policies, standards, structures, processes, and guidance established to ensure that MBS's information resources support the University's mission, goals, and regulatory requirements.

## Governance Bodies

### IT Executive Council (ITEC)

The IT Executive Council serves to discuss IT issues, resources, and challenges at the executive level. Membership includes senior leadership from across the University.

**Responsibilities:**
- Strategic IT direction and prioritisation
- Resource allocation and funding decisions
- Institutional IT risk oversight
- Policy endorsement

### IT Board of Directors (IT BoD)

The IT Board of Directors reviews IT operations, proposals, and projects to advise the CIO on:

- Demand management and portfolio prioritisation
- Strategic alignment of IT investments
- Value delivery and performance measurement
- Policy and procedure development
- Risk management

### Information Security Advisory Council (ISAC)

ISAC provides advisement, review, and endorsement of MBS's Information Security plan, including:

- Security policies and standards
- Strategic security initiatives
- Security services and tooling
- Awareness and training programs
- Incident response oversight

### Technical Advisory Groups (TAGs)

TAGs are chartered to address specific technical domains:

| TAG | Focus Area |
|---|---|
| Cloud TAG | Cloud architecture, migration, governance |
| Data & Analytics TAG | Data platforms, analytics, reporting |
| Integration TAG | API strategy, middleware, ETL |
| Endpoint TAG | Device management, EUC, mobility |
| Network TAG | Network architecture, SDN, Wi-Fi |

## Policy Framework

Policies define the rules and standards that all staff, faculty, and affiliates must follow. They are approved by the IT Executive Council and reviewed annually.

```
Policy (high-level rules)
  └── Standard (specific mandatory requirements)
       └── Guideline (recommended practices)
            └── Procedure (step-by-step instructions)
```

## AT and LMS Governance

Academic Technology and Learning Management System governance ensures that teaching and learning technologies align with pedagogical needs and institutional standards. This includes oversight of the LMS platform, classroom technology, and academic software lifecycle.
