Incident Management

Restore normal service operation as quickly as possible and minimise business impact.

Incident Classification

Priority Impact Urgency Response SLA Resolution SLA
P1 Critical business processes down Immediate 15 min 4 hours
P2 Major feature unavailable High 30 min 8 hours
P3 Minor feature issue Medium 4 hours 24 hours
P4 Informational / cosmetic Low 1 business day 5 business days

Workflow

1. Detection

  • User reports via portal, email, or phone
  • Automated alert from monitoring system
  • Team member observation

2. Logging

  • Create ticket with: user info, description, time, system affected
  • Assign incident ID
  • Categorise and prioritise

3. Investigation & Diagnosis

  • Assign to appropriate team member
  • Document findings in ticket
  • Escalate if unresolved within SLA

4. Resolution & Recovery

  • Apply workaround or permanent fix
  • Verify service restoration
  • Update ticket with resolution details

5. Closure

  • Confirm with reporter that issue is resolved
  • Conduct satisfaction survey (P1-P2)
  • Document lessons learned (P1 only)

Escalation

  • Functional escalation: P1 unresolved after 2 hours → ICT Manager
  • Hierarchical escalation: P1 unresolved after 4 hours → Executive

Major Incident Protocol (P1)

  1. Assemble incident team in dedicated channel
  2. Appoint incident commander
  3. Regular updates every 30 min to stakeholders
  4. Post-incident review within 48 hours
  5. Root cause analysis within 5 business days
