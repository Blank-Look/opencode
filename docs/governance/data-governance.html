Data Governance

MBS relies upon vast amounts of data to facilitate the University's mission and objectives. To ensure that data is managed as a material asset, the University has adopted a Data Management Standard that outlines the roles and responsibilities needed to assure that institutional data provides value, compliance requirements are met, and risks are managed appropriately.

Why Data Governance

Data governance enables:

  • Clarity and consistency in decisions about data access
  • Security measures needed to protect data types
  • Data quality considerations for value and integrity
  • Regulatory compliance through standardised controls

Governance Roles

Role Responsibility
Data Trustees Senior officers with oversight and policy-level responsibility for defined institutional data sets. Work with the CIO to ensure appropriate resources support data needs.
Data Stewards Council Cross-functional council responsible for classifying data, reviewing standards, resolving stewardship issues, and reporting to Data Trustees.
Data Custodians Technical staff who implement and maintain the security controls and infrastructure that protect institutional data.
Data Owners Individuals responsible for specific data sets — determining access, quality, and retention.

Data Stewards Council

The Data Stewards Council (DSC) is the primary operational body for data governance at MBS.

Membership includes representatives from:

  • Academic Affairs
  • Research Administration
  • Finance & Administration
  • Information Technology Services
  • Legal / Compliance
  • Institutional Research
  • Advancement
  • Student Affairs

Responsibilities:

  1. Classifying institutional data in accordance with the Data Management Standard
  2. Ensuring consistent terms and definitions for key data elements
  3. Reviewing data governance standards to ensure consistent treatment
  4. Identifying systems of record for institutional data
  5. Reporting effectiveness of data management practices to Data Trustees
  6. Coordinating and resolving data stewardship issues across functional units
  7. Escalating compliance and risk issues to the Data Trustees

Data Domains

Domain Description Trustee
Student Admissions, enrolment, academic records Provost
HR Employee records, payroll, benefits VP Human Resources
Finance Budget, accounting, procurement CFO
Research Grants, proposals, compliance VP Research
Advancement Alumni, donations, fundraising VP Advancement
IT Infrastructure, systems, security CIO

Data Management Standard

The Data Management Standard establishes:

  • Data classification schema and handling requirements
  • Roles and responsibilities for data stewardship
  • Access control and authorisation procedures
  • Data quality and retention requirements
  • Compliance and audit obligations

Contact its@mbs.edu for access to the full Data Management Standard.
