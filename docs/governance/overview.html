Governance

MBS relies on a strong governance framework to ensure that information resources support the University's mission, goals, and regulatory requirements. Our governance model comprises two complementary domains:

Data Governance

Managing data as a material asset — roles, standards, classification, and stewardship to ensure data provides value, compliance requirements are met, and risks are managed.

Learn about Data Governance →

IT Governance

Policies, standards, structures, and processes that ensure IT investments and operations align with institutional strategy and regulatory obligations.

Learn about IT Governance →

Governance Framework

Our governance model follows COBIT 2019 and ITIL 4 principles, adapted for a university setting:

Domain Focus Key Bodies
Data Governance Data quality, classification, access, stewardship Data Stewards Council, Data Trustees
IT Governance Policy, investment, risk, strategy alignment IT Executive Council, IT Board of Directors
Information Security Threat management, awareness, incident response Information Security Advisory Council
Compliance Regulatory obligations, audits, policy adherence Compliance Lead, Internal Audit
